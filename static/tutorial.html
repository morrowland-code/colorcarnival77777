Strawbebby will guide you through photorealistic coloring step by step.

Step 1: Build your palette

  • Go to the Palette page.
  • Upload a photo of your pencil color chart.
  • Click each swatch, name it, and save it into a palette.
  • You can create multiple palettes (for different brands/sets).

Step 2: Prepare your drawing

  • Print your reference image at the size you want to draw.
  • Print a blank white page grid from the Grid page on a transparent or thin paper.
  • Tape the grid lightly on top of your drawing paper and use a lightbox or window.
  • Make sure everything is aligned and taped so it doesnâ€™t move.

Step 3: Use the grid overlay & matches

  • Upload the same reference image on the Grid page.
  • Choose a grid size that roughly matches your printed grid.
  • Draw the grid overlay digitally and optionally save grid data to the backend.
  • Select a palette and hit Match colors to preview top 5 pencil choices per square.
  • Premium unlock lets you see mix percentages for the top 5 colors.
  • Starting at the top left corner square and moving to the right along the x-axis is the order of the matches.

Step 4: Adjust pressure

  • Use the Pressure page to compare ideal hex vs actual pencil result.
  • The bar shows how hard to press (0% = super light, 100% = very firm).
  • Always build up slowly; realism comes from layered saturation, not just one heavy pass.
